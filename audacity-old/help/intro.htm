Introduction

Set Your Sounds Free with Audacity, the free, fast, powerful cross-platform audio editor!

Audacity is currently in version 0.95. While it is very usable and has many useful features, it is still in beta and is not quite finished yet. Please email your bug reports to dmazzoni@cs.cmu.edu and check the website frequently for new versions. Version 1.0 should be available by Summer, 2001. Audacity is a collaborative project, so if you would like to get involved in any way, check the website for opportunities!

http://www.cs.cmu.edu/~music/audacity/

What can you do with Audacity?

Audacity is a program that lets you manipulate digital audio waveforms. In addition to letting you record sounds directly from within the program, it imports many sound file formats, including WAV, AIFF, AU, IRCAM, MP3, and Ogg Vorbis. It supports all common editing operations such as Cut, Copy, and Paste, plus it will mix tracks and let you apply plug-in effects to any part of a sound. It also has a built-in amplitude envelope editor, a customizable spectrogram mode and a frequency analysis window for audio analysis applications.

What can't you do with Audacity?

Although Audacity is a very powerful audio editor that lets you work with an unlimited tracks of virtually unlimited size, it cannot do everything:
  • Audacity cannot record more than two channels at once. It does not support any special hardware, but just works with your system's default audio device.
  • Audacity opens MIDI files, but it is not a MIDI editor, and its MIDI features are extremely limited.
  • Audacity cannot resample audio to different sample rates (yet).
  • Audacity currently only works with 16-bit quality audio (so far - support for higher quality audio is planned).
  • Audacity cannot export MP3 files (yet).
